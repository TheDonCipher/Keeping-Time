Chapter 1: Your First Ten Mistakes

So you just jacked in. You can feel the hum of the world in your bones and you think you're hot stuff. You're not. You're a liability.

  1. Tuning Too Loud: A high Amplitude with low Coherence is a bright target. Keep it tight. Keep it focused. (Taro: Noise isn't loudness, it's complexity.)
  2. Ignoring the Beat: Every place has a rhythm. Sync with it, then bend it. Fighting the local rhythm is suicide.
  3. Forgetting Your Counter-Melody: Always have a plan B. Pros will read your lead frequency in seconds. Think in phrases.
  4. Mistaking Noise for Chaos: Real Noise artists are masters of controlled chaos. Find the sliver of pattern in their static.
  5. Holding a Note Too Long: You're leaving yourself wide open. Strike, release. Push, pull.

Chapter 2: Street-Level Sonics

TIP: USE THE ECHO
In any urban environment, sound bounces. Use it. Throw a pulse left, hit right. An echo is a free sensor array if you know how to listen.
TIP: FIND THE WEAK NOTE
Everything has a resonant frequency. Tap, listen, find the structural weak spot. A clean note cuts deeper than a muddy one.

Grounding? Carrying a small, tunable resonator is better than guessing. Select a simple 4/4 beat as your internal anchor. When your Coherence frays, fall back to that beat.
