# KEEPING TIME
## Chapter 11: The Wire

### [TIME: UNKNOWN] THE WIRE

There was no up. There was no down. There was only noise. I was in an unindexed zone, reality rendering suspended as my system integrity hit zero percent. The core logic had failed, the standard physics engine offline. Biometric links and neural links were null. Status: <em>OFFLINE</em>. Unfiltered acoustic data flooded my senses, the threshold unbounded and raw.

Taro tried to scream, but "screaming" was a concept that required a medium, and the medium was gone. The Score port at the base of his skull was a white-hot coal, trying to process a world that had ceased to render.

<em>GEOMETRY_ENGINE: OFFLINE.</em> 3D mesh calculation failed, environmental signal loss hitting one hundred percent. SDI: 1.00, HIQ: 1.00. Phase Drift Coefficient: 1.00. Signal Floor: -240 dB.

He was falling through the architecture of reality. Without the Score to render the geometry of the alleyway, the brick walls were just meaningless data points. They didn't look like walls. They looked like jagged tears in the fabric of his perception.

And then—

Silence. Not the screaming void-silence of the alley. A different silence. Heavy. Compressed. The silence of a system powering down to bare minimum.

Taro opened his eyes.

<em>SAFE_MODE: ENGAGED.</em> Emergency recovery protocol was active, processing load restricted to five percent. Resolution dropped to 180p, surface mapping offline and textures disabled. SDI: 0.00, HIQ: 0.00. Phase Drift Coefficient: 0.00. Score Units: 0 SU.

It was [[Safe Mode::A digital purgatory, a stripped-down version of reality where the world's beauty is erased to save the system's soul.]] . And it was terrifying.

Because Taro could feel the wrongness. Time moved differently here. Every breath took an eternity. The drift between intent and action was measured in stuttering microseconds that felt like hours. He was a sketch of a person in a world that had forgotten how to draw.

He tried to sit up. His body was wireframe too. Just a skeletal mesh of glowing blue edges.

"Ven?" he whispered. His voice had no echo. It fell flat against the wireframe walls and died.

No. Wait. There was a sound. Beneath the silence, behind it, cutting through it like a dental drill through enamel—

<strong>EEEEEEEEEEEEEE—</strong>

A carrier wave. The raw, unprocessed signal of the Score port's background hum. Without the audio filters to mask it, it was piercing. Maddening. A tinnitus built by God.

Taro clapped his hands over his ears. It didn't help. The sound was internal. Neuro-acoustic feedback.

Movement in the wireframe void. Another mesh. Orange vertices. Blocky. Heavy. Ven.

"Taro!" Ven's voice sounded compressed. Low bit rate. "Don't move! You're running high, Taro! Your buffer is screaming!"

How long had he been in this void? Seconds? Hours? His internal sense of time was gone. Without the Grid to pace him, without the city's heartbeat to sync to, he was just… drifting. A signal without a frequency.

"How do I get out?!" Taro shouted into the silence.

"You don't!" Ven's mesh moved closer. "This isn't a place, Taro! This is a social shackle! The Score locked you down to stop you from crashing the sector!"

Taro stared at him. "What?"

"You're too loud!" Ven roared. "Your signal is pegged at 300%! The Score port put you in Safe Mode to protect the rest of the city from <em>you</em>!"

It wasn't a life-support system. It was a containment protocol.

He tried to stand. His legs buckled. Vertices collapsed. <em>POLYGON_COLLAPSE.</em> Structural integrity of his mesh was failing, the system recommending he remain still to minimize vertex movement.

"I can't stay here," Taro whispered.

"You don't have a choice!" Ven said. "Your firmware is fried! Voss will come for you. They'll debug you. Just… stay flat."

But "staying flat" in Safe Mode wasn't like waiting in the real world. Time stretched. Every second felt like a minute. Every minute felt like an hour. The carrier wave drilled deeper into his skull.

He was trapped. Stripped of texture. Stripped of sound. Stripped of self. Reduced to geometry. He felt cold. He felt flat. He felt like a sketch of a person, waiting to be erased.

And somewhere in the distance, he heard the heavy, rhythmic thrum of high-fidelity engines approaching. <em>AUDIT_SHIP_01.</em> An authorized C-Order vehicle was identified, an administrative audit imminent. Threat: <em>TOTAL_DELETION</em>.
