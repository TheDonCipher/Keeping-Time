And it was terrifying. Even to me.

Because Taro felt the wrongness. Time executed differently here. Every single respiratory cycle took an eternity to log. The drift between intent and action was measured in stuttering microseconds that dragged like agonizing hours. He was a low-poly sketch of a person in a world that had forgotten how to draw.

Beneath the silence, behind it, cutting through my disabled filters like a dental drill through enamel—

How long had he been suspended in this void? Seconds? Hours? My internal chronometer was corrupted. Without the Grid to pace him, without the city's rigid heartbeat to sync to, he was just… drifting. A signal without a master frequency.
I was losing him.

"How do I get out?!" Taro shouted into the silence.

"You don't!" Ven's orange mesh shifted closer. "This isn't a place, Taro! This is a social shackle! The Score locked you down to stop you from crashing the sector!"

"You're too loud!" Ven roared. "Your signal is pegged at 300%! The Score port put you in Safe Mode to protect the rest of the city from *you*!"
