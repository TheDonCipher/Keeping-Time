HISTORICAL_CONTEXT

The Korelian Steppe was once a vibrant grassland, home to nomadic tribes who practiced a form of communal Rhythm resonance. Their culture was built on the "Heartbeat of the Land," a slow, powerful cadence that informed their traditions and societal structure.

The collision began with the expansion of the Silver Empire, led by Lord Valerius—a Melody virtuoso. He saw the nomads' collective resonance as a primitive drone, an empty canvas. Chieftain Bor, the nomads' most powerful Engineer, rose to defend their way of life, viewing Valerius's intricate Melody as a "chaotic screech" that threatened to shatter their world.

THE_ENGAGEMENT

Chieftain Bor established a monolithic wall of Rhythm—a beat so powerful and unyielding it could supposedly shatter mountains. Bor's Coherence was absolute. Against this, Lord Valerius unleashed a storm of Melody. He wove intricate, razor-sharp melodic phrases designed to find micro-fractures in Bor's cadence.

For three days and nights, the forces were locked. Bor's Rhythm was the ultimate defense; Valerius's Melody was the ultimate attack. Neither could gain purchase. They were the immovable object and the unstoppable force made manifest.

RESONANT_STASIS_ANALYSIS

The catastrophe occurred when both Engineers pushed their Bio-Plasma fields beyond the point of sustainable output. Bor sought to solidify his Rhythm into a concept as permanent as time. Valerius tried to elevate his Melody into a law of physics. The result was a catastrophic Feedback loop.

Their frequencies locked into a state of permanent oscillation. The landscape vitrified, turning miles of grassland into smooth, black glass. The most dangerous artifact of this event is localized temporal shearing. Time within the Glass Plains does not flow linearly. Travelers can experience seconds that last for hours, or days that pass in a blink. To stand on the plains is to hear the argument of Rhythm and Melody, forever unresolved.
