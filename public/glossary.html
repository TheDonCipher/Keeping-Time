Glossary

World-Building Reference
Core Physics
Bio-Plasma
A bio-electric field generated by the nervous system that fuels all resonant manipulation. Unique to each user, acting as a cryptographic key for the World OS.
Coherence (COH)
A percentage value representing the stability of a user's Bio-Plasma field relative to the Local Reality Server. At 0%, the user crashes (coma or death).
The Score (AR Interface)
A visual overlay generated by the NAI that translates invisible acoustic physics into readable graphic data (waveforms, heat maps, text tags). The Score is a representation, not reality itself.
Neural-Acoustic Interface (NAI)
The hardware/software hybrid implanted in all citizens that allows them to perceive and manipulate resonance. It renders "The Score" and mediates interaction with reality.
System States
Safe Mode (BIOS Level)
An emergency protocol where the NAI strips all high-fidelity textures, rendering the world in basic wireframe polygons (blue lines on black) to save processing power. Cold, sterile, and described as "living inside a blueprint."
High-Fidelity (Hi-Fi)
Unfiltered reality rendering. Shows structural stress, micro-expressions, and biological imperfections. Extremely high CPU load—"too loud" for most users. Typically reserved for Admin-class users.
The Void (Source Code)
A total render failure. Raw, unformatted data. Non-Euclidean static, "texture missing" purple/black checkerboards, and absolute chaos. Causes nausea, vertigo, and primal fear.
Standard (C-Order Default)
The default "smoothed" reality. Social lubricants active. Decay hidden. Clean, golden, idealized textures (marble and gold aesthetic).
Failure Modes
Desync (Phase Failure)
Occurs when a user's internal frequency drifts too far from the local environmental baseline. Symptoms include nausea, motor control "lag," sensory artifacting (double vision, pixels), and inability to execute techniques.
Burnout (Neural Scarring)
Permanent reduction in Bio-Plasma capacity caused by channeling Amplitude beyond safe limits. Irreversible. Cumulative burnout results in "Hollowing"—total loss of ability to interface with the Medium.
Feedback (Amplitude Failure)
Runaway amplification. Each action amplifies the next uncontrollably until collapse. Power feeds on itself, often resulting in environmental damage or user burnout.
Resonance Dysphoria
A psychological rejection of one's assigned Class or Frequency. The "Hull" (avatar) feels heavy or tight. The C-Order treats it as "Corruption." The Underground sees it as a sign of a hidden "True Frequency" that needs to be modulated.
Technology & Gear
Limiter
A hardware governor that caps a user's resonant output at safe levels (approx 200 Watts). Tampering with it causes "Unbounded" expression and rapid self-destruction.
Hull (The Mask)
The projected "Corrected Self" avatar that the NAI overlays on a user's physical body. When internal stress exceeds hull capacity, the avatar tears, revealing unformatted biological data underneath.
Faraday Cage (Dead Zone)
A copper mesh enclosure that blocks electromagnetic fields and C-Order Network signals, creating a "Dead Zone" where the NAI cannot function. Illegal. Building one is considered "Treason against the Hive Mind."
Modulation (Illegal)
Unauthorized code injection that modifies a user's resonant frequency, bypassing Hull locks. Penalty: Immediate expulsion and "Reformatting" (Memory Wipe).
Factions & Zones
The C-Order (Unison)
The governing faction. Philosophy: Stability equals Survival. Uses "Damping" and "Anchoring" to prevent change. Their aesthetic is medical/sterile (white and gold). They view Dissonance as a disease to be cured.
Twilight Band (Sector 4)
The buffer zone between the High-Fidelity Inner City and the Low-Fi Outer Slums, characterized by inconsistent reality rendering. Textures pop in and out. The air tastes of both ozone (wealth) and sulfur (poverty).
The Silence (The Agents)
A black-ops unit that doesn't use Resonance but consumes it. Their "Null-Blades" absorb 100% of incident energy. If you are "Silenced," you don't die—you are erased from the server entirely.
The Underground (The Null)
A sanctuary for Dissonant users in the Sub-Bass levels (Level 4). A Faraday-caged club where High-Fidelity is rejected and raw noise is worshiped. Here, "Factory Settings" are suggestions.
Admin Class (The Architects)
High-level system overseers. They don't "Resonate"—they "Edit." An Admin can delete a user's ID tag with a gesture, effectively erasing them from society. They see the code behind the curtain.
