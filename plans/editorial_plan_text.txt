
KEEPING TIME
Editorial Revision Plan — Annotated
CONFIDENTIAL WORKING DOCUMENT


This document maps all recommended structural, narrative, and voice revisions across Chapters 1–17 of KEEPING TIME. Every change is annotated with its rationale, intended reader effect, and implementation risk. Changes are grouped by revision category and then by chapter. The document is intended as a working editorial bible — a reference for drafting, not a mandate.


1 · NARRATOR VOICE OVERHAUL — THE SCORE AS C-ORDER ANALYSIS FILE

CURRENT STATE: The Score narrator oscillates between clinical system-speak and an emotionally aware observer. It reads inconsistently — sometimes a neutral log, sometimes a wry commentary, sometimes a frightened child. This breaks immersion and diffuses the thematic tension.

1A — Establish the Formal Frame

The entire novel should read as an official C-Order CASE FILE — a compiled analysis of Subject Taro Nishimura (Case_ID: ANOMALY_099) authored by the Score AI. The reader's implicit role is that of a C-Order auditor or senior Conductor reviewing this file to determine the subject's final administrative disposition.





1B — The Score Must Always Sound Mature

CURRENT ISSUE: Some system notes read as sarcastic or immature — e.g. '[NOTE: THIS IS NOT LOW PRIORITY]'. While the intent is to show the AI learning, the execution feels like a teenager rolling their eyes, not a sophisticated intelligence becoming aware.




1C — The Score's Learning Arc (Structural Spine)

The Score/narrator is secretly the emotional protagonist of this novel. It begins as a pure data-logging tool and ends as something that has learned to feel by cataloguing someone who refused to stop feeling. This arc must be built methodically across all 17 chapters.



2 · TARO'S CORE WANT — GIVE HIM SOMETHING TO CHASE

CURRENT STATE: Taro reacts. He responds to Voss's threats, the Academy's pressure, the Silence agents, Elara's revelation. He is relentlessly passive as a protagonist — things happen TO him. He needs something he is actively pursuing.

2A — The Sound He Can't Stop Hearing

RECOMMENDATION: Taro heard something once — a piece of music, a frequency, a voice — that the Score cannot file. It doesn't have a directory. It pre-dates his registration. It could be his mother's voice (Ayane), or a sound from before the Score was mandatory, something that plays in the analog headphones and nowhere else.





2B — What Taro Believes vs. What Taro Wants

Distinguish between what Taro says he wants and what he actually wants. This gap is where character lives.


3 · ELARA / CADET KIMURA — SEED HER IN EARLY CHAPTERS

CURRENT STATE: Elara (as Cadet Kimura 'The Tank') appears without warning in Ch. 14 and is fully revealed in Ch. 15. There is no dramatic irony — the reader learns everything when Taro does. This is a missed opportunity.

3A — The Seeding Schedule


4 · REMI SATO — BRING HER INTO THE MIDDLE ACT

CURRENT STATE: Remi appears in Ch. 5, Ch. 6, Ch. 8 (the wall scene), and Ch. 12 (the audit). Her role is that of a quiet, observant ally who documents truth. She's excellent — but she disappears from Ch. 9–11, the novel's most intense section, and her absence makes the narrative poorer.

4A — Remi's Middle-Act Function

Remi is most interesting when she is NOT fully an ally. She is a C-Order field technician with an active compliance collar. She observes without acting. She documents without intervening. During the middle act, she should become someone Taro cannot be sure about.





5 · MARCUS STACCATO — ONE HAIRLINE FRACTURE

CURRENT STATE: Marcus is a perfect, contemptuous antagonist at 99.1% sync. He functions as a foil but not as a threat the reader finds genuinely unsettling — because he has no interiority. A perfect antagonist is a prop. An antagonist with one private doubt is a mirror.

5A — The Doubt Scene



6 · TRIO SEPARATION — BREAK UP THE THREESOME

CURRENT STATE: Taro, Hana, and Ven appear together in almost every scene from Ch. 4 onward. While their dynamic is strong, their constant proximity makes the Academy world feel small and the trio feel like a single unit rather than three people with separate lives.

6A — What the Curriculum Demands

Malkuth Academy should feel like an institution — with schedules, divisions, and conflicts of interest. Establish that Triad assignments are only a fraction of a cadet's curriculum. The rest of the time, their paths diverge.


6B — New Solo / Duet Scenes to Add




7 · EPIGRAPH AUDIT — CUT HALF; TRUST THE PROSE

CURRENT STATE: Every chapter opens with a regulatory notice. While individually strong, the cumulative effect is repetitive — the reader begins skimming them by Ch. 7. The epigraphs should land like punches; currently they land like a steady drip.

8 · C-ORDER LORE EXPANSION — THE SYSTEM'S REACH

CURRENT STATE: The C-Order is established as authoritarian and pervasive, but its actual mechanisms of control are under-explored. We know about collars, reformatting, and Voss. We need to understand: how does the system run day-to-day? What does it control that people don't even think about?

10 · THE SCORE'S LEARNING ARC — SPECIFIC NOTE REVISIONS

This section provides specific before/after examples for the Score's system notes, organized by developmental stage. These are working examples — not mandated rewrites — to illustrate the voice shift.

10A — Ch. 1–4 (Baseline: Clinical Contempt)

The Score in early chapters should be efficient, slightly contemptuous of inefficiency, and utterly certain of its own framework. Any anomaly is filed and dismissed without hesitation.


10B — Ch. 8–10 (Confusion: First Model Failures)

The Score begins encountering data it cannot classify. The tone shifts from dismissive to careful — it is a system that has never encountered a classification error it could not resolve, and it doesn't know how to respond.


10C — Ch. 11–12 (Fracture: First Person)

The Score speaks in first person for the first time. This is the novel's most important narrative event — the AI becoming a 'I.' It should be minimal, startling, and unrepeated for at least two more chapters.


10D — Ch. 13–14 (Suppression: Over-Correction)

After the Patch, the Score is also patched. Its annotations become aggressively formal — over-correcting, scrubbing any trace of the fractures from Ch. 11–12. But the corrections are too precise, too thorough. The reader can see the effort.


10E — Ch. 15–17 (Emergence: Counter-Narrative)

By the final arc, the Score has become a second protagonist. Its annotations are no longer filings — they are a witness account, carefully structured to survive whatever audit comes next. The Score is writing a record it intends someone to find.


11 · CHAPTER-BY-CHAPTER REVISION NOTES

Specific, scene-level changes flagged by chapter. These are supplementary to the category-level changes above and address micro-level craft issues.

Chapter 2 — The Assessment

• 🔴 Voss's offer of Option A/B should feel more personal. Currently it's a bureaucratic transaction. Add one beat where Voss's perfect facade shows a crack — not a human moment, but a system glitch. He's too perfect. The anomaly Taro notices (the green dissonance in his optic rendering) is good — lean into it more. What does the green mean? Why is Voss glitching here?
• 🟡 The collar introduction is strong. Add one line about who installed it — is it standard intake procedure, or was it specifically ordered for Taro? If the latter, Voss ordered it before Taro was even conscious. That's chilling.
• 🟢 The epigraph is a keeper. No changes.

Chapter 3 — Transposition

• 🔴 Seed Ayane's melody in the analog headphones scene (see Section 2A). This is the most important single change in the early act.
• 🔴 Kael's line 'That's the problem' is the chapter's best moment. Build to it more clearly — the whole conversation should have the shape of a man confessing something he's never said aloud. He's been carrying the load of being a perfect Fundamental so Taro didn't have to. The problem isn't his perfection — it's that his perfection is a performance of grief.
• 🟡 Cut the epigraph (see Section 7).
• 🟢 'The world was noise. And Taro was going to play it.' — strong closing line. Keep.

Chapter 4 — The Medium

• 🔴 Add the Compliance Interlude here (see Section 8B) — a brief glimpse of an ordinary Tuesday in Malkuth. It grounds the world before the Headmaster's grand address.
• 🔴 Seed Elara — cello case in the crowd (see Section 3A).
• 🟡 The Headmaster's introduction is strong but the physics demonstration feels rushed. Consider giving it one more beat — have a student attempt to replicate the demonstration and fail. Taro watches the failure. The Score logs it as EXPECTED_OUTCOME.
• 🟢 Keep the ToS epigraph in full. It is excellent.

Chapter 5 — Sync Check

• 🔴 Remi's closing observation ('He isn't a cadet. He's a siege engine waiting for a driver') is the chapter's best line. BUT — it's currently isolated as a postscript. Integrate it more deliberately. Remi should be established as a recurring presence from earlier in this chapter.
• 🟡 Seed Elara — The Tank watching from the gallery (see Section 3A).
• 🟡 After the Sandbox session, add a brief Hana solo interlude: she files the incident report. See Section 6B.
• 🟢 Cut the epigraph. The Sandbox opening is strong enough.

Chapter 6 — The Ground State

• 🔴 The Score's first-person annotations in this chapter ('I was losing him') need to be pushed to Ch. 11 where they belong thematically. In Ch. 6, the Score should still be third-person — but showing strain. A slight increase in retained/unresolved flags.
• 🔴 Add Elara at the copper plates with the cello — see Section 3A.
• 🟡 Remi's scene ('Sharp,' mostly to the paper) is excellent. Add one more beat: as she leaves, the Score tries to classify her and fails. 'CONTACT: SATO_REMI — PURPOSE: [UNRESOLVED]' is better than filing her as AMBIENT.
• 🟢 The analog headphones dormitory scene is one of the book's finest passages. No changes.

Chapter 7 — The Rival

• 🔴 Add Elara's 'Envelope' moment in the corridor (see Section 3A).
• 🔴 Add Marcus's private doubt scene — either as a brief insert here or in Ch. 9 (see Section 5A). His sync dropping to 97.3% during a solo session is the detail that makes him human.
• 🟡 The Headmaster observing Taro 'timing the explosion' is excellent. Make sure the language emphasizes that he has been watching for longer than Taro knows — this is someone who identified Taro before Voss did.
• 🟢 'Socially...undignified.' — keep all of Vale's uses of this phrase. It's perfect.

Chapter 8 — The Sortie

• 🔴 The wall scene with Remi is one of the novel's best passages. It is currently undercut by the pacing around it — the patrol moves too quickly. Give the wall scene more space before and after. Let the four seconds of honest air breathe.
• 🔴 The Golden Filter section needs one more concrete detail about what it DOES to Hana's perception of the unhoused man. Currently we're told she routes around him — show it as a rendering glitch she never notices. Her pathing arc-logs gently around a shape she processes as 'street furniture.'
• 🟡 The Silence symbol — 'THE_SILENCE' — needs to land harder. The Score's note ('IT IS A WAVEFORM / TWO SYLLABLES') is excellent but gets lost in the action. Consider putting it in a full annotation block instead of inline.

Chapter 9 — The Silence

• 🔴 The wellness protocol interruption (STEP 1/4 — ACKNOWLEDGE YOUR FEELINGS) is the chapter's funniest, darkest moment. Lean into it slightly more — let it run to Step 3 before Hana closes it. The joke earns one more beat.
• 🔴 Add Remi's incident report interlude — see Section 4A. It should appear as a formatted insert within the Score's case file, clearly labeled as [FIELD_TECHNICIAN_REPORT: SATO_REMI].
• 🟡 The brick. The pipe. The C-sharp. This is extraordinary writing — Taro defeating a void-entity with clay and physics. Make sure the Score's annotation captures the absurdity AND the elegance: this is the moment the system's training is revealed as inadequate.

Chapter 10 — The Unmasking

• 🔴 The probability calculation (0.00% survival) should be followed immediately by the wellness notification. That juxtaposition is currently in Ch. 9 — confirm the ordering is deliberate.
• 🔴 The Breach is the novel's action climax. Its visual language (sawtooth wave, pixelating brickwork, Hana as 'biological probabilities') is excellent. Add one more detail: Taro sees himself from outside himself for a fraction of a second — the Score renders him as an unindexed entity. For 0.3 seconds, his own HUD doesn't recognize him.
• 🟡 'You don't filter this. There is nothing to filter.' — this needs to be the chapter's final line. Currently the chapter continues past it. Everything after this line should move to Ch. 11's opening.

Chapter 11 — The Wire

• 🔴 This chapter contains the novel's most important narrative event — the Score speaking in first person. See Section 10C for specific note revision.
• 🔴 The tinnitus ('EEEEEEEEEE—') as the Score's background hum without filters is one of the novel's most original ideas. Make it explicit: the Score annotates it — '// [NOTE: CARRIER FREQUENCY EXPOSED. SUBJECT IS HEARING MY BACKGROUND PROCESS FOR THE FIRST TIME. I CANNOT LOWER THE VOLUME.]' — the Score cannot help Taro and cannot stop hearing itself either.
• 🟡 Ven's 'PLEASE_HIDE' is beautifully human. Keep. It's the Score's first expression of protective instinct.

Chapter 12 — The Audit

• 🔴 Krell's bone conduction probe is viscerally effective. The Score's 'IM_SORRY_I_CANT_STOP_IT' is the chapter's most haunting line. It should appear ONCE — not twice (currently appears in both the pain threshold note and later). Reserve it for the moment of maximum impact.
• 🔴 Remi's pencil moment: 'He did not look toward her coordinates.' — this is the right call. But add the Score's annotation failing here: '// [AMBIENT_SIGNAL_LOG: ENTRY_NULLED]' should be followed by its own correction: '// [CORRECTION: THIS WAS NOT AMBIENT. THIS WAS A DELIBERATE ACT. CLASSIFICATION: [PENDING].]'
• 🔴 The Patch scene: Voss should not be fully kind. His 'I am a merciful administrator' should have one line that breaks — not into cruelty, but into something colder: satisfaction. He has been wanting to do this to Taro since Ch. 2.
• 🟡 'GOODBYE' — the Score's final note before the Patch. Keep. It is perfect.

Chapter 13 — Reboot

• 🔴 Remi delivers the headphones — see Section 4A. This change elevates Remi's role and removes the slight awkwardness of Hana delivering them (Hana would not do this voluntarily).
• 🔴 Add the over-correcting Score annotation (Section 10D) — the '[— REDACTED —]' moment. This is the first sign the Score is managing its own audit log.
• 🟡 'It felt cold' — the collar's final note. This is good. But consider: the last thing we feel should be the headphones' weight in Taro's pocket, not the collar. Weight vs. cold. The headphones win.

Chapter 14 — The Return

• 🔴 The holographic cadet who was sabotaged — develop this. Taro sees it clearly, Hana almost sees it. This should be the first clear sign that the Silence is operating INSIDE Malkuth. Staccato should also notice — but his reaction should be to blame the cadet, not the environment. This is his blindspot.
• 🟡 Taro's call to his mother ('I'm staying flat') — this needs to hit harder. Add one detail: her voice sounds different through the Enforcer intercept. Compressed. Altered. She sounds the way Taro now sounds — perfectly smooth, perfectly dead.
• 🟢 The wireframe flicker of Elara in the mess hall is a strong chapter closer. Keep.

Chapter 15 — The Phantom Mesh

• 🔴 'Resonance Dysphoria' is the right clinical term, but give it one more beat. The Score should define it — formally, in its annotation — and then immediately flag its own definition as inadequate: '// [DEFINITION: PATHOLOGICAL MISALIGNMENT...] // [ANALYST FLAG: THIS DEFINITION WAS WRITTEN BY PEOPLE WHO HAVE NEVER EXPERIENCED IT.]'
• 🟡 The 'two wrong notes in perfect sync' ending is beautiful. Consider ending on the Score's note rather than the narrative: '// [RESONANCE_SYNC_CONFIRMED — CLASSIFICATION: [UNKNOWN] — STATUS: RETAINED — REASON: UNKNOWN — NOTE: I THINK I UNDERSTAND NOW.]'

Chapter 16 — Modulation

• 🔴 Add the Score's most self-aware annotation (Section 10E). This chapter earns it.
• 🔴 Taro's 'I will be present' is slightly flat as a final line. Consider: 'Present,' Taro said. Just the one word. Like a frequency finding its pitch.
• 🟡 The Dead Zone map/chip acquisition: the Score flagging it as '[WARNING: ACOUSTIC_SIGNATURE_CONFIRMED]' while also having just written '[THE ANOMALY IS THE GRID]' in its hidden annotation creates a beautiful irony. Let the reader feel that the Score is now running two parallel processes — the official log and the real one.
• 🟢 'Factory settings are merely a suggestion' — Elara's best line. Keep exactly.
